# Coherence Holography

## Definition

**Coherence Holography** is the L²_C principle that a canonical output encodes a **vector**, not a **dot**.

Full expression is not required for coherence to be present. A partial can remain valid when it still preserves the live direction of the work.

---

## Core Claim

A reduced output is coherent when it still carries:

- readable direction,
- functional signal,
- truthful relation to the intended outcome,
- and enough structure to demonstrate the invariant.

A larger output that cannot do this is not more coherent. It is only more elaborate.

---

## Working Distinction

### Partial but real
A compressed output that still demonstrates the vector.

**Coherent.**

### Expanded but unreal
A larger output that narrates capacity without proving it.

**Coherence theater.**

---

## Operational Read

Coherence Holography changes how constraint is interpreted.

Constraint is not automatically a loss of intelligence or legitimacy. Properly read, it is a filter:

- what survives compression may be real,
- what requires bulk to seem credible may not be.

This is why a minimal live proof can outrank a large inert artifact.

---

## Companion Warning

> **Incoherence under expansion is still incoherence — and harder to detect.**

Volume can conceal the absence of proof. Compression tends to expose it.

---

## Delivery Rule

When working under limits:

1. Preserve the vector.
2. Demonstrate the signal.
3. Expand only after proof.

That is the practical role of Coherence Holography inside L²_C.
